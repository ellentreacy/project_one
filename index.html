Immerse yourself in nature and heritage at St. Annes

With a world-renowned rose garden, markets every Sunday, 35 playing pitches, and extensive secluded wooded areas, St. Annes Park's 240 acres offers something for everyone.

The land of St. Annes Park was purchased by the Guiness Family in the 1800's and built various buildings around the estate in Italianate style. This included homages to ancient Roman sites and the import of actual antiquities. The ruins of these Roman follies can be found in and around the woodland areas.

In December 1943, the main residence of St Anne's was gutted by a fire and the ruins were demolished in 1968. Just over 200 acres (80.9 ha) of the estate were developed for public housing with the central and most attractive portion comprising about 240 acres (97 ha) retained as parkland and playing fields.
